Watching, Listening and Reading Highlights of the Week - Part 0

This is a weekly post containing cool references (preferably links in the hyperspace) of things I watched, read, saw or in general want to share but don't warrant their own post

Feel free to send me things you think I might enjoy as well. My e-mail can be found here.

Watching

Warning: Please be mindful that YouTube says that when you click on the video-links, YouTube will collect your user data, so please exercise your right to not open a video if you care about this.

I've watched this amazing good video-essay by Sarah Davis Baker. It puts into words that feeling that we lost when we moved to the "new internet", which is the soul-lacking, algorithmically-driven internet we have today in counterpart to the internet of the late 90s and early 2000s, which I assure you was truly personal, authentic and best of all: not ubiquitous.

Another great video I saw this week was an almost two-hour-long compilation about the life and historical events of each and every Pope. I'm sure as you all know Pope Francis just died and it's very interestign to look back at how the papacy has been an active player in world history and politics since even before catholicism was a word. Worth the watch, albeit very concise, due to time constraints, I assume.

Reading

For my reading, I recommend following this hyperlink to Uros Popovic's Bare metal printf - C standard library without OS. It's nothing super new for me as I've significantly dabbled into microcontrollers before, but it's a nice refresher. The target for the printf function is the UART16550 connected to an emulated riscv64 machine.

Listening

KEXP has a new AURORA performance up on their channel which has been on repeat for the past two months. It's pretty amazing to see how her vocal range has evolved over the years. I'm a bit biased about her earlier stuff, but she manages to always impress.
